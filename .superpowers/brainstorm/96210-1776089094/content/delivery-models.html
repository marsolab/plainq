Subscription Delivery Models

Same topic/subscription architecture, different consumer mechanics

A

Lease + Ack

Consumer receives messages, broker leases them for a visibility timeout, consumer explicitly acks, and unacked messages are redelivered.

Feels like: RabbitMQ queue semantics on top of durable subscriptions.

Best for: workers, retries, DLQ, operational safety.

Trade-off: more delivery-state writes and more broker complexity.

B

Offset Commit

Consumer reads by offset and periodically commits progress. Replay is native, but per-message redelivery control is weaker.

Feels like: Kafka consumer groups.

Best for: stream processors, analytics, fast sequential readers.

Trade-off: less natural fit for queue-style retry and DLQ handling.

C

Hybrid

Topic retains data by offset, but subscriptions can optionally use lease-based delivery semantics for processing workflows.

Feels like: one core log plus two consumption modes.

Best for: broad product surface and future flexibility.

Trade-off: most flexible, but also the hardest v1 to keep coherent.
